People who inspire

Malala Yousafzai, Nobel Prize Laureate

Malala Yousafzai, a Pakistani activist and Nobel laureate, has an awe-inspiring journey that began with her passionate advocacy for girls' education in her homeland. In 2012, at just 15 years old, Malala was targeted by the Taliban for her outspoken views and was shot in the head. Miraculously, she survived and continued her activism, becoming a global symbol of courage and education.

Sophie Cruz, Immigration Activist

Sophie Cruz, a young activist from Los Angeles, has become a symbol of hope and change in the United States. Born to undocumented Mexican immigrant parents, Sophie embarked on a remarkable journey at just five years old when she made headlines by delivering a heartfelt letter to Pope Francis during his visit to the United States in 2015. In the letter, she implored the Pope to advocate for immigrant rights and family unity.

Yusra Mardini, UNHCR Goodwill Ambassador

Yusra Mardini, a Syrian refugee turned Olympic swimmer, inspires kids worldwide with her incredible resilience and determination.Her journey began when she and her sister fled war-torn Syria, embarking on a perilous journey to Europe. Along the way, their boat faced engine failure in the Aegean Sea, putting their lives at risk. Despite being a competitive swimmer in Syria, Yusra had to rely on her swimming skills to help push the boat and save the lives of her fellow passengers. This act of bravery not only saved the lives of those on board but also caught the world's attention.

Ishmael Beah, UNICEF Advocate for Children Affected by War

Ishmael Beah is a Sierra Leonean author and human rights advocate who emerged from a harrowing journey as a child soldier during the country's civil war in the 1990s. Ishmael's journey from child soldier to a globally recognized advocate for child soldiers' rights and the reintegration of former child soldiers into society is deeply inspirational. Through his work and storytelling, he raises awareness about the devastating impacts of war on children and strives to prevent other children from enduring similar experiences.
